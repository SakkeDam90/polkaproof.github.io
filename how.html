How PolkaProof Works

PolkaProof is designed to be simple in use and strict in rules. This page explains how the protocol works without requiring technical knowledge.

1. Everything happens on-chain

PolkaProof consists of immutable smart contracts deployed on a public blockchain. Once deployed, the rules cannot be changed, paused, or overridden.

There are no administrators, no special permissions, and no hidden controls.

2. No identity is required

The protocol does not store names, documents, emails, or personal information. Participants interact through cryptographic addresses only.

Trust is derived from what can be verified on-chain — not from who someone claims to be.

3. Rules are enforced automatically

All constraints — such as locked balances, vesting schedules, and fixed supply — are enforced directly by smart contracts.

If a rule exists, it is applied equally to everyone, without exception.

4. Transparency without disclosure

Anyone can inspect the state of the protocol through public blockchain explorers. This provides transparency without requiring users to reveal their identity.

Verification replaces trust. Proof replaces promises.

5. The protocol works without its creators

PolkaProof does not depend on a team, company, or operator to keep running. Even if the website disappears, the on-chain contracts remain active and verifiable.
