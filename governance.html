Governance by Design

Governance in PolkaProof is intentionally limited. The protocol is designed so that core guarantees do not depend on votes, people, or promises.

What governance can do

Governance in PolkaProof can only be used for predefined, non-critical decisions, such as community parameters, optional modules, or ecosystem-level initiatives.

Governance operates strictly within boundaries enforced by smart contracts.

What governance cannot do

Certain elements are permanently excluded from governance:

• Founder vesting and sale limits
• Token supply constraints
• Core protocol guarantees
• User privacy assumptions

No vote can override these rules. This is enforced by code, not trust.

Why governance is limited

Unlimited governance often leads to instability, power concentration, or social pressure. PolkaProof avoids this by making critical rules immutable.

Governance exists to coordinate — not to control.

Autonomy first

Once deployed, PolkaProof does not require active management. Governance is optional, transparent, and always secondary to the protocol itself.

If governance disappears, the protocol continues to function exactly as designed.
