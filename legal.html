Legal & EU MiCA Positioning

PolkaProof is designed as neutral, open-source infrastructure. This page explains how the project is positioned in relation to the EU’s Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) in a clear, non-technical way.

1) What PolkaProof is (and is not)

PolkaProof is an autonomous protocol implemented as immutable smart contracts. It provides publicly verifiable on-chain transparency and proof mechanisms.

PolkaProof is not an exchange, broker, custodian, identity provider, or compliance service. It does not collect personal data and does not perform KYC.

2) How MiCA generally applies

MiCA primarily targets entities that provide crypto-asset services (e.g., trading platforms, custody, brokerage), or entities that exercise control over offering, promotion, or operation of services.

In practice, regulatory obligations are focused on parties with operational control, intermediaries, or customer-facing services.

3) Why PolkaProof is positioned as neutral infrastructure

PolkaProof operates without privileged roles and without administrative control: no admin keys, no upgrade mechanisms, and no discretionary overrides.

Following deployment, the protocol functions autonomously. Users can verify protocol state on-chain, without relying on trust in individuals or organizations.

4) Exchanges and listings

If the PPRF token is listed on third-party platforms, those platforms make independent decisions and apply their own compliance processes (including KYC/AML) where required.

PolkaProof does not operate exchanges, facilitate trading, or provide financial services. Any listing is the responsibility of the exchange or platform offering that service.

5) Disclaimer

PolkaProof provides no guarantees, no financial advice, and no compliance assurances. Users interact with the protocol at their own discretion and rely on publicly verifiable on-chain data.

This website is informational and may evolve over time. The protocol logic is enforced by the deployed smart contracts.

Note: This section is provided for transparency and general understanding only and does not constitute legal advice.
